ABOUT US


Richmond Point Capital Limited (Richmond) was established in 2015 with the view to help governments modernize their infrastructure and develop their Natural Resources industries, with the objectives to derive better revenues from existing facilities, and to improve access to the International Capital Markets to raise adequate Project & Trade Finance.


Richmond also specialises in analysing and structuring Investments opportunities for private corporations in the fields of energy and natural resources. The company is based in Richmond (UK), with a branch office in Paris.


Richmond has developed a specific knowledge in assessing assets and liabilities with a focus on large infrastructures, energy, and mineral resources. It aims to optimise the Debt / Asset mix for its clients. By so doing, Richmond intends to enhance the client’s chances to tap a larger array of financial markets, reducing its debt servicing burden and increasing its levels of reserves.




Who we are.
We are a group of humans with a shared goal


Tony Littlejohns
Senior Partner & Co-Founder

Tony Littlejohns has some 25 years experience in the commodities sector, in 2005 he established, funded and led a Western European based private commodity group of companies under the Evolve Group banner. These companies were principally active within the oil and gas sectors, and metals and diamond industries, with affiliated companies in Western Europe, Middle East and Russia, and eventually expanded into soft commodity trades.

In parallel to the above, he was active in emerging markets with satellite offices in South America. He also invested in a broad spread of start-up or developing companies, including ventures in software development, motorsport, and property, with an objective of future sales.


Roger Crowe
Senior Partner & Co-Founder

Roger Crowe has some 40 years broad based experience including qualifying as a Chartered Accountant. Beginning with leading accountancy firm, Deloitte, his experience grew to encompass major international and merchant banking operations on three continents, and includes high-level management consultancy, and the international insurance industry.

Since 2006 Roger has operated as a partner in a private project finance and development company.


Michel Bachelier
Partner

He joined the company in 2018, with the view to capitalise on his experience in structuring and lead managing large projects in the infrastructure sector. He has been fairly active in both developed economies and emerging markets. Before joining Richmond Point Capital Ltd, he was the Senior Partner of an independent consulting firm, Trellis Capital Ltd (which became later Trellis UK Ltd) which main focus was to advise Governments and international operators on their natural resources and energy projects.

Prior to that, he was Managing Director of Lehman Brothers, Global Head of International Project Finance. Mr Bachelier also worked on a number of Corporate Finance and Structured Finance transactions, as General Manager of Kleinwort Benson in France. Previously, he worked at Banque Indosuez as the Global Head of Project Finance.

Mr Bachelier holds a BA in Economics from La Sorbonne University and an MBA from Harvard Business School. Mr Bachelier graduated as an engineer from ENSAM in France.




Services
Richmond intervenes through a series of different mandates that may coverany of the following areas


Investment Banking & Corporate Finance

• Analysing and restructuring the Liability Structure of Government owned or private industries. • Raising Debt and Equity from International Financial Institutions. • Structuring Pre-Export Finance transactions based on tradable assets, including Natural Resources.



Advisory Services

• Assisting Governments in the re-negotiation of International Treaties relevant to the Natural Resources. • Assisting Governments in restructuring the existing Foreign Debt. • Assisting corporations on the structuring and negotiation of Project Finance transactions.



Business Strategy & Corporate Governance

• Developing the local Capital Markets and assisting in setting up a modern financial local infrastructure. • Assisting Clients in defining new approaches to the International Financial Markets. • Helping in the negotiations of Credit Facilities with the view of widening the access to financial instruments, which are currently not available for certain countries.
